## 1. Source-line parametrization

Use the predecessor notation

\[
D=\langle\omega\rangle,\qquad
\Sigma=\{a,b,c\},\qquad B=D\setminus\Sigma,
\]

where \(|D|=36\), \(|B|=33\), and

\[
F=\mathbb F_{17}[u]/(u^2+16u+3).
\]

For \(i\in B\), set

\[
r_i^{(b)}=\frac{b-D_i}{a-D_i},\qquad
r_i^{(c)}=\frac{c-D_i}{a-D_i}.
\tag{1.1}
\]

For a 12-root core \(C\subset B\), put

\[
g_b(C)=\prod_{i\in C}r_i^{(b)},\qquad
g_c(C)=\prod_{i\in C}r_i^{(c)}.
\tag{1.2}
\]

The canonical source coefficients generated by \(C\) are

\[
\alpha(C)=g_c(C)\frac{b-c}{b-a},\qquad
\beta(C)=\frac{g_c(C)}{g_b(C)}\frac{c-a}{b-a}.
\tag{1.3}
\]

All factors are nonzero.  Conversely, (1.3) recovers \(g_c\) from \(\alpha\)
and then \(g_b\) from \((\alpha,\beta)\).  Thus the map from core product
states \((g_b,g_c)\) to normalized source lines \((\alpha,\beta)\) is
injective.

The exact dynamic program below finds all

\[
(|F|-1)^2=288^2=82{,}944
\]

nonzero product pairs at core size 12.  Hence every pair
\((\alpha,\beta)\in(F^\times)^2\) occurs, although many distinct cores may
have the same product state.

## 2. Fixed-\(\Gamma\) coordinate-cover lemma

Fix a source line and a finite post-deep slope set \(\Gamma\).  For each
\(\eta\in\Gamma\), let \(\mathcal W_\eta\) be the complete family of
compatible 13-root locators, and define

\[
\mathcal A_\eta
=\bigcup_{S\in\mathcal W_\eta}S.
\tag{2.1}
\]

For a complete selector \(\sigma\), the predecessor's exact witness
classification gives

\[
E^\sigma_\eta=B\setminus S^\sigma_\eta.
\]

Consequently

\[
\bigcup_{\eta\in\Gamma}E^\sigma_\eta
=B\setminus\bigcap_{\eta\in\Gamma}S^\sigma_\eta.
\tag{2.2}
\]

Let

\[
c_*(\Gamma)=
\max_\sigma
\left|\bigcap_{\eta\in\Gamma}S^\sigma_\eta\right|.
\]

Because \(|B|-R=33-22=11\),

\[
\boxed{
\kappa_*(\Gamma)=\max(0,11-c_*(\Gamma)).
}
\tag{2.3}
\]

Selector choices are independent across slopes.  Therefore

\[
\boxed{
\kappa_*(\Gamma)\le10
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\bigcap_{\eta\in\Gamma}\mathcal A_\eta\ne\varnothing.
}
\tag{2.4}
\]

This singleton criterion is specific to \(k=14\).  For larger \(k\), the
threshold requires common extendable \((k-13)\)-subsets rather than individual
coordinates.  Also, several coordinates lying in every
\(\mathcal A_\eta\) do not imply that one selector contains all of them
simultaneously.  Equation (2.4) uses only one fixed coordinate.

The LOW conclusion is monotone under deleting slopes: a selector on the full
post-deep envelope restricts to any earlier-owner survivor.  The HIGH
conclusion is not monotone in that direction.

## 3. Exact product-pair and availability dynamic program

For cardinality \(s\le13\) and a product pair \((x,y)\in F^2\), the verifier
stores

1. the exact number of \(s\)-subsets with products \((x,y)\); and
2. a 33-bit mask equal to the union of coordinates occurring in any such
   subset.

Starting from the empty state \((s,x,y)=(0,1,1)\), coordinate \(h\) updates
cardinalities downward by

\[
(s-1,x,y)
\longmapsto
\left(s,xr_h^{(b)},yr_h^{(c)}\right).
\tag{3.1}
\]

Counts add and availability masks are ORed with the bit for \(h\).  Since
each ratio is nonzero, multiplication is a permutation of the \(289^2\)
product-pair state space.  Descending cardinality prevents coordinate reuse.

The replay checks

\[
\sum_{x,y}N_{12}(x,y)=\binom{33}{12}=354{,}817{,}320,
\]

\[
\sum_{x,y}N_{13}(x,y)=\binom{33}{13}=573{,}166{,}440.
\tag{3.2}
\]

It also checks the recurrence by brute force on a six-coordinate,
three-subset fixture and replays the complete table in reverse coordinate
order, including bit reversal of every availability mask.

For a source line \((\alpha,\beta)\), a post-deep witness at slope \(\eta\)
has product state

\[
\left(\eta,\alpha+\beta\eta\right).
\tag{3.3}
\]

The two excluded values are exactly \(0\) and \(-\alpha/\beta\).  At every
other slope both coordinates of (3.3) are nonzero.

## 5. Owner consequence

Fix any core-derived source line and choose, for example, \(h=3\).  Section 4
shows that for every post-deep slope \(\eta\) there exists a compatible
locator \(S_\eta\) containing \(3\).  Selecting one independently at every
slope gives

\[
\bigcup_\eta E_\eta
\subseteq B\setminus\{3\},
\]

so

\[
\kappa_*\le |B|-1-R=32-22=10.
\tag{5.1}
\]

The existing excess-ten carrier cap is

\[
B_{10}
=\left\lfloor
\frac{\binom{32}{11}}{\binom{11}{10}}
\right\rfloor
=11{,}729{,}498>287.
\tag{5.2}
\]

Thus all 287 post-deep slopes on every one of the 82,944 source lines exit at
or before the proved low-carrier owner.  The two source-zero slopes are the
same exact extended-deep exceptions as in the predecessor argument.  Hence
the complete 289-slope finite toy frontier closes at deep or at-or-before low
carrier for every core-derived source line.

No literal earlier-owner partition is needed for this LOW conclusion: any
such partition only deletes slopes, and the fixed-coordinate selector
restricts.  No empty-cover or unpaid primitive source line survives to rank
nine in this ansatz.
