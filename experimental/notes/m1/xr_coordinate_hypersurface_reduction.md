# XR Coordinate Hypersurface Reduction

Status: PROVED.

Source DAG node: `xr_coordinate_hypersurface_reduction`.

## Statement

Work on a fixed XR light-triangle profile chart with the normal-form matrix
from `xr_triangle_eliminant_form`.  Let `E` be the stacked twisted-sum matrix,
with columns indexed by the three shortened dual spaces.  Coordinate-special
stagnation is the rank-drop locus

```text
rank E < 3t.
```

Equivalently, all `3t x 3t` maximal minors vanish.

Assume the profile has at least one maximal minor whose cleared chart
representative

```text
Delta
```

is not the zero polynomial.  Then the coordinate-special stagnation locus in
that profile chart is contained in the proper hypersurface

```text
Delta = 0.
```

The degree is bounded by the cleared determinant degree of the chosen minor,
which is fixed by the normal-form construction and the chart.

## Proof

The normal-form construction gives a matrix `E` whose rank-drop locus is
defined by the determinantal ideal generated by its maximal minors.  Therefore
every coordinate-special stagnation point vanishes on every maximal minor.

Choose one maximal minor with nonzero cleared representative `Delta`.  Since
the coordinate ring of the profile chart is a polynomial ring modulo the
chart's distinctness/open conditions, a nonzero `Delta` does not vanish
identically on the chart.  Its zero set is therefore a proper hypersurface in
the Zariski closure of the chart, and the rank-drop locus is contained in that
zero set.

Clearing the Vandermonde chart denominators does not change the rank-drop set
inside the chart, because the cleared factors are nonzero on the chart.  The
degree bound is the degree of this cleared determinant, hence is explicitly
bounded once the chart and minor are printed.

## Program Use

Together with `xr_light_profile_eliminant_nonvanishing.md`, this removes the

This is a local M1/XR residual input.  It is not a row-level adjacent upper
certificate and does not count the points on the hypersurface.  The population
