## 2. Global tangent owner

Let \(\Gamma_{\rm in}\) be the exact set of bad finite slopes remaining after
the globally earlier owners.  Define the fixed-translation tangent image

\[
 \mathcal T(\epsilon_0,\epsilon_1)
 =\left\{-\frac{\epsilon_0(x)}{\epsilon_1(x)}:
 x\in\Sigma,\ \epsilon_1(x)\ne0\right\}
\tag{2.1}
\]

as a set, not a multiset, and put

\[
 Z_{\tan}=\Gamma_{\rm in}\cap
            \mathcal T(\epsilon_0,\epsilon_1),\qquad
 \Gamma_{\rm out}=\Gamma_{\rm in}\setminus Z_{\tan}.
\tag{2.2}
\]

### Theorem 2.1 (global fixed-translation tangent owner)

For every fixed SP3 translation (1.3),

\[
 \boxed{|Z_{\tan}|\le |\mathcal T(\epsilon_0,\epsilon_1)|
                    \le |\Sigma|\le j.}
\tag{2.3}
\]

The set \(Z_{\tan}\) is a globally valid first-match owner on the sparse
mutual layer, charged once per received pair.  The outgoing family is the exact
set difference in (2.2).  Every later owner, including the residual base-slope
universe, is applied only to \(\Gamma_{\rm out}\).

### Proof

Each coordinate \(x\in\Sigma\) with \(\epsilon_1(x)\ne0\) produces exactly
one finite field element
\(-\epsilon_0(x)/\epsilon_1(x)\).  Coordinates with zero denominator produce
none, and repeated ratios are identified because (2.1) is a set.  Hence
\(|\mathcal T|\le|\Sigma|\le j\), and intersection with
\(\Gamma_{\rm in}\) proves (2.3).

The MCA numerator counts distinct bad finite slopes for one received pair.
SP3 fixes one translation for that entire pair and preserves its bad-slope set.
Thus (2.3) is one uniform numerator charge, not a sum over witnesses or
translations.  Intersection with \(\Gamma_{\rm in}\) makes the owner disjoint
from every earlier owner, and the exact deletion in (2.2) makes it disjoint
from every later owner.  Later uniform caps remain valid on the smaller
residual. \(\square\)

The predecessor zero-pencil theorem gives

\[
 Z_0(\epsilon_0,\epsilon_1)\subseteq\mathcal T(\epsilon_0,\epsilon_1).
\tag{2.4}
\]

Consequently no slope in \(\Gamma_{\rm out}\) admits an eligible translated
zero-codeword witness.  In any later canonical rich-pencil atlas built from
the same translation, a line with \(P_L=Q_L=0\) would consist of such
zero-codeword witnesses.  Therefore every contributing residual rich line has

\[
 (P_L,Q_L)\ne(0,0).
\tag{2.5}
\]

The zero-pencil subset receives no separate charge.
