\[

\[

## 5. Sufficient coverage routes

The readiness manifest records three sufficient mathematical routes.  It does
not claim that these exhaust every logically possible future proof design.

### Route S: source-bound paying selector

A source-bound construction may choose one paying complete rank-nine selector
for every eligible received/source family, verify the full fields in Sections
3--4, and prove (1.5) for every constructed selector.  The owner is existential
over complete selectors, so this route does not require all selectors or a
rank-minimizer unless a later complementary-residual claim needs one.  One
supplied partial selector or one unbound source line does not instantiate this
route.

### Route U: uniform theorem

A proof may quantify over every eligible exact-\(A\), noncontained source
family and prove that at least one complete rank-nine selector has (1.5),
without enumerating the deployed field.  Proving the bound for every complete
selector is sufficient but not required.

### Route C: exhaustive census

Alternatively, a machine producer may enumerate every source family, exhibit
at least one paying complete selector for each, reconstruct (3.1)--(4.6), and
deduplicate graph-line ownership.  Exhausting the whole selector universe is
needed only if the certificate also claims rank-minimizer status or a
complement in which every selector fails.

One source line, one partial selector, sampled supports, support moments, or a
toy exhaustive census satisfies no route.  A theorem certificate that merely
sets a Boolean flag also satisfies no route; its load-bearing proof must be
independently reviewed and source-bound.
