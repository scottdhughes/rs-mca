## 1. Exact theorem

Use the deployed constants

```text
p  = 2^31-1 = 2147483647,
N  = 2^21   = 2097152,
m  = 981129,
w  = 67447,
d0 = N-w    = 2029705,
B* = 2^24-1,
L  = B*+1   = 16777216,
R  = m(N-m) = 1094962529967.
```

Let `F` be a family of `L` distinct `m`-subsets of the actual `N`-coordinate
M31 domain, all in one depth-`w` weight-plus-power-sum prefix fiber. Suppose
the exchange distance between every two distinct members belongs to
`{t,2t}`. Then

```text
t >= 277868  implies that F does not exist.                 (1)
```

Equivalently, the source-grid predicate

```text
kappa=2 and t>=277868, with (e1,e2)=(t,2t),                (2)
```

is a sound exclusion classifier. On the finite source grid it marks exactly
`212,697` rows, from `(2,277868,277868,555736)` through
`(2,490564,490564,981128)`.

## 2. Integral Gram matrix and prefix rank

Let `X` be the `L x N` binary incidence matrix of `F`. Join two family
members by an edge when their distance is `t`, let `A` be the adjacency
matrix, and put

```text
Q=2I+A.
```

Since two incidence rows at distance `d` have inner product `m-d`, entrywise

```text
tQ=XX^T+(2t-m)J.                                          (3)
```

All rows have weight `m`, so `1_L=m^(-1)X1_N` over both the rationals and
`F_p`. Hence the column space of `J` lies in that of `X`. The common
weight-plus-`w`-moment prefix and the M31 Vandermonde rank theorem give

```text
rank_Fp(X)<=N-w=d0.
```

The same column-space observation over the rationals also gives
`rank_R(Q)<=rank_R(X)<=N`, which is used only in the determinant upper bound.

Every source-grid `t` and `m` is nonzero modulo `p`, so (3) yields

```text
rank_Fp(Q)<=d0.                                            (4)
```

This is a modular rank cap only; no modular-to-real rank identification is
used.

## 3. A common-height ADE root system

Center the incidence rows by

```text
y_S=1_S-(m/N)1_N.
```

Their Gram matrix satisfies

```text
tQ=YY^T+(2t-R/N)J.                                        (5)
```

For `t>=277868`, one has `2Nt-R>0`, so `Q` is positive semidefinite. Set

```text
h^2=2-R/(Nt)>0.
```

After adjoining a unit vector `e` orthogonal to the centered incidence
space, define

```text
alpha_S=t^(-1/2)y_S+h e.
```

Then

```text
<alpha_S,alpha_T>=Q_ST,
||alpha_S||^2=2,
<alpha_S,alpha_T> in {0,1} for S!=T.                      (6)
```

Let `V` be their real span and let `zeta=h^(-1)P_V e`. Every selected root
has common height

```text
<alpha_S,zeta>=1,
||zeta||^2<=rho(t):=Nt/(2Nt-R).                           (7)
```

The integer span `Lambda` of the `alpha_S` is an even integral positive
definite lattice in `V`. It is discrete: choose a maximal independent subset;
every other generator has rational coordinates obtained by inverting its
integral Gram matrix. Reflections in norm-two vectors preserve `Lambda`.
Because `Lambda` is generated by its norm-two vectors, it is an orthogonal
sum of simply-laced root lattices of type `A`, `D`, and `E`. Equation (7)
also places `zeta` in the dual lattice.

## 4. Common-height root-count lemma

We use the following finite lemma.

> **Lemma.** Let `S` be roots in an ADE root lattice of total rank `r`, with
> distinct pairwise inner products in `{0,1}`. Suppose a dual vector `zeta`
> has `<alpha,zeta>=1` for every `alpha in S` and
> `||zeta||^2<=rho`, where `8<rho<17/2`. Then
>
> ```text
> |S| <= rho*r+4*rho*(16-rho).                            (8)
> ```

For completeness, decompose the lattice and `zeta` over irreducible
components. For an irreducible root system of Coxeter number `H`,

```text
sum_{beta in Phi}<beta,z>^2=2H||z||^2.                   (9)
```

Each selected height-one root and its negative contribute two, so a component
with squared dual height `Z_C` contains at most `H Z_C` selected roots.

For `A_s`, this is at most `(s+1)Z_C<=rho*s+Z_C`. The `E` components have
`H<=30`, giving at most `rho*r_E+30Z_E` in total.

For `D_s`, use roots `+/-e_i+/-e_j`. If the dual coordinates are integral,
at most eight are nonzero because `Z_C<=rho<9`. Counting supports meeting a
nonzero coordinate, with pairwise nonnegativity, gives fewer than `rho*s`
selected roots. If the coordinates are half-integral, then `s<=4Z_C`.
There is at most one selected root on each coordinate support. A matching of
size `q` gives `q` orthogonal roots, so Bessel and the height-one condition
give `q/2<=Z_C<17/2`, hence `q<=16`. A graph on `s` vertices with matching
number `q` has at most `qs`; therefore this component contributes at most

```text
16s<=rho*s+4(16-rho)Z_C.
```

Summing the component bounds and using `4(16-rho)>30` proves (8).

## 5. Exact rank gap and determinant contradiction

The function `rho(t)` decreases with `t`. At the boundary

```text
rho0=rho(277868)=582731431936/70500333905,
8<rho0<17/2.
```

Apply (8) to the `L` selected roots. Since their span has real rank
`r=rank_R(Q)`,

```text
r >= ceil(L/rho0-64+4rho0)
  = 2029720
  = d0+15.                                                (10)
```

Choose `r` independent selected roots and let `B` be their nonsingular
principal Gram block. By (4), `rank_Fp(B)<=d0`; Smith normal form gives

```text
p^(r-d0) divides det(B),
det(B)>=p^15>2^450.                                       (11)
```

The independent roots generate an ADE sublattice. If it has `c`
irreducible components, choose one basis root in each. These roots are
orthogonal and have `zeta`-height one, so Bessel gives `c/2<=rho0<17/2` and
therefore `c<=16`.

The irreducible determinants are

```text
det(A_s)=s+1, det(D_s)=4,
det(E6)=3, det(E7)=2, det(E8)=1.
```

If the component ranks are `s_1,...,s_c`, then

```text
det(B)<=product_j(s_j+1)<=(r+1)^16<=(N+1)^16<2^352,      (12)
```

contradicting (11). This proves (1).
