# F97 first-interior line: exact two-cell first-match certificate

## Status and claim

`PROVED-SPECIAL / EXACT FINITE CERTIFICATE` for one pinned
`F_97 / mu_16` line.  The retained first-interior LineRay subincidence has
exactly four witnesses.  Two fixed-domain-root common-GCD cells cover those
four witnesses, and ordering the cells gives an exact first-match slope budget
of three.

The cell claims are not inferred from the three chosen generators.  After the
common GCD is divided out, the verifier enumerates the entire residual
projective plane in two ways and proves that its split-locator intersection
contains exactly the three stated locators.

This is a finite fixture certificate.  It is not a general first-match atlas,
a tangent classifier, an extension-field result, a deployed-row estimate, or
an asymptotic closure of `prob:saturated-bc`.

vector span, hence a projective plane over `F_97`.  For each plane the verifier


## First-match conclusion

The witness membership and slope projections are

```text
C_A cap R = {z0,z1,z2a},       pi(C_A cap R) = {0,1,2},
C_B cap R = {z0,z1,z2b},       pi(C_B cap R) = {0,1,2}.
```

Their union is all four retained witnesses.  Ordering `A` before `B` in the
first-match sense of `def:first-match` gives

```text
Z_A^o = {0,1,2},
Z_B^o = {0,1,2} \ Z_A^o = empty,
|Z_A^o|+|Z_B^o| = 3.
```

Any cells placed earlier can only delete slopes, so the same three-slope upper
bound remains valid for this retained subincidence.  This is the local input
shape used by `lem:first-match-bound`; it does not assert that `A,B` classify
every possible cell on arbitrary lines.
