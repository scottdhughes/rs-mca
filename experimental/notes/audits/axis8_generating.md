This packet resolves the S0 axis-8 question: the official family does not
exclude non-generating rows.  Therefore tower/intermediate-field cases are
admissible objects and must be priced by the extension-lift or F1
classification chain rather than silently discarded.

## Statement

ABF26 defines the challenge for Reed-Solomon codes `RS[F,L,k]` with smooth
`L subset F`.  It imposes smooth-domain, rate, dimension, and field-size
conditions, but it does not impose:

```text
F = field generated by L.
```

Consequently, if a valid smooth subgroup `L` lies in a proper subfield `B` of
an admissible ambient field `F`, then `RS[F,L,k]` is still inside the stated
family.  This is the non-generating/tower case.

```text

```

The inference is structural: the listed constraints are constraints on the
ambient field, subgroup domain, rate, and caps; they do not add a generated-
field equality condition.

## Consequence

Field-ledger packets should treat the non-generating case as live:

```text
generating row:       q_gen = q_line/ambient field, no tower gap
non-generating row:   q_gen can be a proper subfield ledger inside ambient F
required response:    price or exclude by a named extension/tower theorem
```

This is why extension-line MCA lift, F1 field descent, and tower-case
classification remain genuine proof obligations in the program.
