### Theorem (the `j+5` over-determination identity) — PROVED, with proof

**Statement.** Fix `ell` and let `mu_1 >= ... >= mu_j >= 3` be any excess=3
atlas shape (so `sum_k(mu_k-2) = E_3 = ell+3` by definition). Demanding that
each `mu_k` be realized as a genuine fiber (all `mu_k` points of one coset
sharing one `Gamma`-value) imposes exactly

> `sum_k (mu_k - 1) = E_3 + j = ell + 3 + j`

linear conditions on `Gamma`'s `ell-1` raw coefficients; against the
`ell-2`-dimensional projective space of candidate `Gamma` (mod the overall
scalar `Gamma ~ c*Gamma`, `c != 0`, which preserves every fiber structure
exactly), this is an **exact excess of `j+5` conditions over the available
dimension** — uniformly over the atlas, independent of which shape.

**Proof.** *(Single fiber, exact codimension — not merely generic.)* Fix one
fiber `F_k = {x_1,...,x_m}` (`m := mu_k <= ell-3`, the atlas's own per-part
cap, hence `m <= ell-1`), all nonzero (a coset of `F_p^*`). Constant-free
`Gamma` of degree `<= ell-1` is a point of `F_p^{ell-1}` (coordinates
`gamma_1,...,gamma_{ell-1}`). Evaluation at the `m` distinct points
`x_1,...,x_m` is **onto** `F_p^m`: given any target `v = (v_1,...,v_m)`, let
`P` be the ordinary Lagrange interpolant of degree `<= m-1` through the pairs
`(x_i,v_i)` (possibly with `P(0) != 0`), and `S(X) := prod_i(X-x_i)` (degree
`m`; `S(0) = (-1)^m prod_i x_i != 0` because every `x_i != 0`). Then

> `Gamma := P - (P(0)/S(0)) * S`

still satisfies `Gamma(x_i) = v_i` for every `i` (since `S(x_i)=0`), has degree
`<= m <= ell-1`, and is constant-free by construction (`Gamma(0) = P(0) -
(P(0)/S(0))*S(0) = 0`). So evaluation at `F_k` is onto `F_p^m`, and "all `m`
values equal" — a corank-`(m-1)` linear condition on the target space `F_p^m`
— pulls back, under a **surjective** linear map, to an **exact**
codimension-`(m-1) = (mu_k - 1)` subspace of `Gamma`-space (exact, because
preimages of subspaces under surjections preserve codimension — not merely an
upper bound, and not merely generic-position).

*(Sum over the shape, then projectivize.)* Summing this exact per-fiber count
over the `j` fibers gives the naive total `sum_k(mu_k-1) = sum_k(mu_k-2)+j =
E_3+j` — pure arithmetic from the shape's own defining equation, requiring no
independence assumption between different fibers' conditions (it is literally
adding up `j` individually-exact numbers). Passing to the projective space of
`Gamma` (dimension `ell-2`, since an overall nonzero scalar never changes
which points collide, hence never changes any fiber) and substituting the
atlas's own `E_3 = ell+3`:

> `overdet := (E_3+j) - (ell-2) = (ell+3+j) - (ell-2) = **j+5**`. `QED`

**What this is, and is not.** The per-fiber codimension count is exact and
unconditional — it holds for *any* `m <= ell-1` nonzero points, realizable
shape or not, no realizability assumed. The *sum* across fibers is honest
arithmetic, not an independence claim: nothing above asserts the `j` fibers'
condition-subspaces intersect generically. "Over-determined by `j+5`" is
therefore an exact, PROVED bookkeeping quantity — but it measures the *naive*
codimension deficiency, i.e. how many algebraic coincidences a realization
needs, **not a non-existence proof**: a realizing `Gamma` is exactly a point
of the (necessarily non-generic) common intersection of the `j` condition
subspaces, and such coincidences are exactly what this problem is about. W3
is the concrete demonstration that they occur: its shape `[14,3^7]` has `j=8`
(excess `+2`, not the atlas's own `+3`), so the general (not `j+5`) form of
the over-det count gives `12` — yet W3 exists (Sec 2). The identity narrows
the atlas to an ordering by "how many coincidences"; it does not itself rule
any shape out — that is exactly why item 6's frontier stays open.
`verify_l1_t7_atlas.py`
gate ii re-derives `overdet == j+5` from scratch on every one of the 1239
shapes at `ell in {17,19}` (the two cheap cases; `--full` extends the atlas
regeneration itself to all five `ell`, and the identity is asserted as a
self-check inside the enumerator at every `ell` it runs).

**(b) The rank / weighted-moment-curve form.** Write `nu(t) = (1,t,t^2)` and the

> **`k3` cosets concurrent at `q` `<=>` the (up to) `3 k3` fiber points' images
> `M(t)` lie on `k3` PARALLEL planes (common normal `q`), 3 per plane, one plane

> `{M(x)-M(y), M(y)-M(z)}_{coset}` all lie in the 2-plane `q^perp`.

## 3. Sharpened ROUTE-CUT: why Bezout and dimension cannot see the cap

- **Value-curve Bezout = Lemma R = `Theta(ell^2)`.** `C_f` has degree `O(ell)`;
  the number of its `>=3`-fold points is `<= delta/3`, `delta = binom(ell-1,2)`,
  i.e. `O(ell^2)` — and equals the Lemma-R budget `(ell-1)(ell-2)/6`. The
  `q`-quadratic restriction cuts the 3-dim linear system `{g_0 q}` inside
  degree-`<=ell-1` polys but leaves `deg C_f` unchanged: it is **codimension,
  not degree**, so no Bezout count improves.
- **Dimension = over-determined by `j+5`, but coincidences realize it.**
  Sec 1's Theorem says every excess=3 shape is over-determined by `j+5`; W3
  (excess `+2`, `j=8`, general-formula over-det `12`, not the excess=3-specific
  `j+5`) shows the cyclotomic coincidences saturate well past the generic
  bound.
- **Verdict.** A `Theta(1)` cap is a statement about which point-sets a single
  degree-`<=ell-1` polynomial can carry as equal-value fibers ACROSS cosets of
  `mu_ell` — the `(W,lambda)`-Veronese transversality the integrated notes flag

  size-`(ell-3)` locator `g_0` admits no `q in P^2` with `>= 8` cosets

  impossible. Open.
