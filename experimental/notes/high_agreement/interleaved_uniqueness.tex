\subsection{Interleaved-list uniqueness}

For \(\mu\ge1\), let \(\Lambda_\mu(C,a)\) denote the maximum, over all
\(\mu\)-row received words \(U=(U_1,\ldots,U_\mu)\), of the number of tuples
\((c_1,\ldots,c_\mu)\in C^\mu\) that agree with \(U\) on at least \(a\) common
positions.

\begin{theorem}[high-agreement interleaved-list uniqueness]
\label{thm:interleaved-unique-high-agreement}
Let \(C\subseteq F^D\) be MDS of length \(n\) and dimension \(k\).  If
\[
  2a-n\ge k,
\]
then for every \(\mu\ge1\),
\[
  \Lambda_\mu(C,a)=1 .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The lower bound is obtained by taking \(U\) itself to be a code tuple.  For the
upper bound, suppose two tuples \(c=(c_1,\ldots,c_\mu)\) and
\(c'=(c'_1,\ldots,c'_\mu)\) both agree with \(U\) on supports of size at least
\(a\).  The supports intersect in at least \(2a-n\ge k\) positions.  On this
intersection, each row \(c_i\) agrees with \(c'_i\).  Since \(C\) is MDS, two
codewords that agree on \(k\) positions are equal.  Thus \(c_i=c'_i\) for every
row \(i\), so the tuples are identical.
\end{proof}
